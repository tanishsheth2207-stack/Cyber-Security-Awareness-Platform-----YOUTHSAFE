Threat Type

Protection Tool

Short for "malicious software". It includes viruses, spyware, and ransomware — harmful programs disguised as normal apps or files that damage your device or steal your data once installed.

Threat Type

Virtual Private Network. It creates a secure, encrypted "tunnel" for your internet traffic so hackers and even your ISP can't see what you're doing online. Especially useful on public Wi-Fi!

Privacy Tool

The process of scrambling data into an unreadable format so only the intended person (with the right key) can read it. WhatsApp messages are end-to-end encrypted — meaning only you and the receiver can read them.

Security Concept

Manipulating people psychologically to reveal confidential information or do something harmful — no hacking needed. Examples: fake "tech support" calls, impersonating a friend online to ask for money, or posing as a school official.

Quick Tips

Your Digital Safety Checklist

Start with these quick wins to dramatically improve your online security today.

01
🔑

Use Strong Passwords

Use a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers & symbols. Never reuse passwords across sites. Use a password manager.

02
📲

Enable 2FA Everywhere

Two-Factor Authentication stops 99% of automated account hacks. Enable it on every account you own.

03
🔗

Think Before You Click

Hover over links before clicking. If the URL looks suspicious or doesn't match the sender, don't click it!

04
🔒

Lock Down Your Profiles

Set your social media to private. Regularly audit who can see your posts, stories, and personal information.

05
🌐

Avoid Public Wi-Fi

Never log into sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi. Use a VPN if you must. Prefer mobile hotspot instead.

06
📢

Report, Don't Suffer

If you're being harassed online, document it and report it — to the platform, your school, or a trusted adult.
